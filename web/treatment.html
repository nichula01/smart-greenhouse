Strawberry Diseases and Treatments

Gray Mold (Botrytis cinerea)

A fungal disease that affects strawberry fruits, flowers, and foliage, especially in wet weather conditions. It appears as gray fuzzy mold on affected plant parts.

Symptoms:
Gray fuzzy growth on fruits and flowers
Soft, brown rot on berries
Wilting flower petals with gray spores
Treatments:
Improve air circulation by proper plant spacing
Apply fungicides like captan or fenhexamid at flowering
Remove infected plant material promptly
Use drip irrigation instead of overhead watering
Mulch under plants to prevent fruit contact with soil
Leaf Spot (Mycosphaerella fragariae)

A common fungal disease affecting strawberry foliage. It begins as small purple spots that develop tan or gray centers as the disease progresses.

Symptoms:
Small purple spots on leaves
Spots develop lighter centers with age
Severe infections may cause leaves to dry and curl
Treatments:
Apply copper-based fungicides or myclobutanil
Remove infected leaves during the growing season
Use disease-free transplants when establishing new plantings
Practice crop rotation (do not plant strawberries in the same area for 3-4 years)
Avoid overhead irrigation to reduce leaf wetness
Anthracnose Fruit Rot (Colletotrichum spp.)

A serious fungal disease that can affect all parts of the strawberry plant. It's particularly destructive to fruits, causing sunken lesions and fruit rot.

Symptoms:
Sunken, dark lesions on fruits
Salmon-colored spore masses in lesions
Crown rot and wilting of entire plants
Dark lesions on stems and petioles
Treatments:
Use resistant cultivars when available
Apply fungicides containing azoxystrobin or pyraclostrobin
Use disease-free transplants
Mulch to prevent soil splashing onto fruits
Remove and destroy infected plants
Rotate crops and avoid overhead irrigation
Angular Leafspot (Xanthomonas fragariae)

A bacterial disease that causes water-soaked lesions on leaves. The name comes from how the spots are typically limited by leaf veins, creating angular shapes.

Symptoms:
Water-soaked, angular lesions on leaf undersides
Spots appear translucent when held to light
Lesions turn reddish-brown with age
Yellow bacterial ooze may be visible in high humidity
Treatments:
Apply copper-based bactericides early in the season
Use disease-free planting material
Practice good sanitation by removing infected leaves
Avoid overhead irrigation and working with wet plants
Rotate crops (3-year minimum between strawberry plantings)
Blossom Blight (Botrytis cinerea)

A form of gray mold that specifically targets strawberry blossoms, preventing fruit formation or causing young fruit to rot. Common in cool, wet spring conditions.

Symptoms:
Brown discoloration on flower petals and stamens
Gray fuzzy growth on blossoms
Blossoms turn brown and die without setting fruit
Infection may spread to developing fruits
Treatments:
Apply fungicides during flowering period
Remove infected blossoms promptly
Improve air circulation with proper spacing
Use row covers to protect plants during wet weather
Time irrigation to allow foliage to dry before evening
Powdery Mildew (Podosphaera aphanis)

A fungal disease that appears as a white powdery coating on leaves, stems, flowers, and fruit. Unlike many diseases, it can thrive in both dry and humid conditions.

Symptoms:
White powdery growth on leaf surfaces
Upward curling of leaf edges
Reddish-purple blotches on leaves
Stunted plant growth
Poor fruit development
Treatments:
Apply sulfur-based fungicides or potassium bicarbonate
Use resistant cultivars when available
Improve air circulation by proper spacing
Remove severely infected leaves
Apply neem oil or other horticultural oils
Avoid excessive nitrogen fertilization
